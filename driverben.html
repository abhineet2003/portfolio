PROCESS HIGHLIGHTS

Brief

Challenge

The challenge was to identify and understand the issues faced by stakeholders in the Driverben Initiative. The task was to analyze the program's impact and effectively communicate these insights through creative mediums to highlight its significance and suggest improvements.

Opportunity

This project provided opportunities to deeply understand the Driverben initiative's impact, creatively represent stakeholder challenges, and offer insights to improve the program.

Responsibilities

  • Conducting field research
  • Stakeholder engagement
  • Impact analysis
  • Issue identification
  • Creative representation
  • Insights and recommendations

PROCESS HIGHLIGHTS

The process

RESEARCH

Field Visits

Our initial observations highlighted safety concerns and societal disapproval affecting female drivers, as well as practical issues like the distance to training grounds. These observations led us to explore the daily realities faced by individuals involved in the program.

RESEARCH

Day-in-the-Life

By examining daily routines, we uncovered how these safety concerns and practical issues directly impact trainees' ability to balance household responsibilities with training. This deeper look showed the personal and logistical challenges trainees face.

Empathy Mapping

This involved capturing their feelings, challenges, and needs to understand their perspectives and identify key emotional and practical issues impacting their experiences with the Driverben program.

DEFINE

Stakeholder Needs

Program Organizers: Need to address challenges faced by all stakeholders and ensure the program's sustainability and impact.

Trainers: Need resources, effective teaching methods, and logistical support to deliver quality training.

Family Members: Seek financial and emotional support to help trainees participate in the program.

Trainees: Require effective training, safety, and support to manage household responsibilities alongside their learning.

Community Members: Desire positive engagement and support for the program to enhance its acceptance.

DEFINE

Key insights

Community Attitudes
Mixed reactions affect program acceptance.

Home Impact
Everything begins at home, highlighting the crucial role of family support in the program's success.

Logistical Barriers
Distance and travel expenses are significant challenges.

Emotional Impact
Societal pressures and responsibilities cause stress.

Family Support
Essential for trainees' success.

Safety Issues
Female drivers face safety concerns and societal disapproval.

IDEATION

Speculative Scenario Building

By creating and analyzing different scenarios, we identified potential impacts and areas for improvement in the program.

IDEATION

Idea

In our ideation process, we scripted our findings and presented them in a diary written in Hindi, adding a homely touch with tea cup stains and actual pamphlets. We adjusted the style to reflect the subject's aging by progressively simplifying the decorations, ultimately creating a more focused and serious presentation to show growth and progression.

OUTCOME

Video Film

Our research journey has been a window into the lives of many, like Amita Ben, who faced challenges but found hope in the Driverben program. In our video, we share a girl's story through a discovered diary, showing her struggles and eventual success as a driver. It's a reminder of the power of programs like Driverben to empower individuals and change lives for the better.
